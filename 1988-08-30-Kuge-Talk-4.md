In other words, various aspects of human life: daily routine, washing your face; those are five petals. The human world.

Vimalakirti says, “No root.” And Shariputra finally asked, “From where does the no root come?” You know? Vimalakirti says no root comes from no root. *[He laughs.]* So still we believe there is *somewhere* no root as a root, from [where] the no root comes. That is our intellect, always.

- Could you say that “no root” just means whatever the circumstances are or conditions? 

If you touch it, you feel empty inside. It's really empty. 

So personally, inside is very alone. Very alone. Nothing to depend. But outside, externally, the person, *I*, as a being, just accept, just follow the Buddha Way. That's it. When the morning comes, just accept morning and follow it, and take care of morning. That's it. But inside is always hollow. Inside is always screaming: “I want to depend on something; I want to *get it*.” But if you are really taken over by this inside emptiness, I think that is called *confusion*. So always you have to pay attention to that feeling of emptiness inside. 

- Called what? Confusion? 

Confusion. Your life is confused by emptiness. Emptiness makes you puzzled, more and more. 

- Makes you puzzled.

Because emptiness is really emptiness; nothing to hold on to. But still we don't like emptiness, because nothing to hold on to, so consciously or unconsciously we want to. So we start to struggle, forgetting something. In *many* ways. You can see it: in many ways we struggle, [and] forget it. 

That makes you confused and complicated again and again, so finally you don't understand the meaning of emptiness, you don't understand the root of your life.

- So you have to return again and again to zazen or just accepting the moment, and let it go. 

Yes, we can say [so]. Day to day, we have to do. But it's not so simple. But we have to do it. 

That's why sutra says, *[unintelligible]*. If you really want to see Buddha with one single heart, you don't spare body and life. Naturally you have to continue to do this again and again, beyond whether you are free from *samsara* or not. Even if you are free from *samsara*, you still continue to practice this. 

So very serious practice, on one side. The other side is very compassionate, soft; the universal, where everything is accepted totally. 

So you cannot stay in either one of them. You should accept *both*. And then work. That is manifesting your life as a Buddha.
